Sinigang

Sinigang is a popular Filipino soup that is known for its sour and savory flavor. It is typically made with meat or seafood, vegetables, tamarind, and other spices. The soup is a comforting and hearty dish that is often enjoyed as a main course during lunch or dinner. Sinigang's sour taste comes from the tamarind, which is used as a souring agent, and it is often served with steamed rice. Its unique sour and savory flavor makes it a staple in Filipino cuisine and a favorite among locals and visitors alike

Ingredients

list of ingredients commonly used in making sinigang:

  • Meat or seafood (pork, beef, chicken, shrimp, fish, etc.)
  • Tamarind (fresh or powdered) or other souring agents like guava, calamansi, or kamias
  • Water
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes
  • Vegetables (kangkong, okra, eggplant, radish, string beans, etc.)
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • Black pepper

Optional ingredients that can be added to give the soup more depth and flavor include:

  • Chili pepper
  • lemongrass
  • Long green peppers
  • Mustard leaves
  • White raddish (labanos)

The exact combination and proportions of ingredients can vary depending on personal preference and regional differences.
