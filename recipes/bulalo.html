Bulalo

Bulalo is a Filipino soup dish made with beef shank and bone marrow, simmered for several hours until the beef becomes tender and the broth is rich and flavorful. It is seasoned with onions, garlic, peppercorns, and fish sauce, and often includes vegetables such as corn on the cob, bok choy, and green beans. The bone marrow is a highlight of the dish, which is scooped out of the bones and added to the soup just before serving. Bulalo is a popular comfort food in the Philippines and is often served with rice and a dipping sauce made with calamansi and soy sauce

Ingredients

The ingredients typically used in bulalo are:

  • Beef shank with bone marrow
  • Water
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Peppercorns
  • Fish sauce
  • Salt
  • Corn on the cob
  • Bok choy
  • Green beans

However, the ingredients may vary depending on the recipe and personal preference. Some people may also add other vegetables such as potatoes or cabbage, and some may include additional seasonings and spices to enhance the flavor of the soup

Steps

Here are the general steps for making bulalo:

  • In a large pot, add the beef shank and enough water to cover it. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  • Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for several hours (around 2-3 hours) until the beef is tender and the broth is rich and flavorful. Skim off any foam or impurities that rise to the surface
  • Add the onions, garlic, peppercorns, and fish sauce to the pot and let it continue to simmer for another 10-15 minutes.
  • Add the corn on the cob, bok choy, and green beans to the pot and continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  • Remove the beef shank and marrow bones from the pot and let them cool slightly. Then, scoop out the marrow from the bones and add it back to the pot.
  • Season with salt to taste.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice and a dipping sauce made with calamansi and soy sauce.

Note: The cooking time may vary depending on the size and thickness of the beef shank. You may need to adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure that the beef is cooked through and tender.
